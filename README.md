### **My Values & Philosophy**

*   **People First**
    I believe people are the most important ingredient in a company’s success. Great people and a healthy culture naturally lead to great products. My focus is on optimizing processes so everyone can perform at their best.

*   **Transparency**
    I will never lie to you. I wouldn’t stay at a company that required me to do so. Expect the truth from me. However, there will be times when I am required to keep information confidential until a specific date (e.g., upcoming promotions or sensitive departures).

*   **No Leader is Always Right**
    There is no silver bullet for leadership, and no one is 100% right all the time. I expect us to give each other continuous feedback. If you think I’ve screwed up, or if there’s something you think I won’t want to hear—please tell me. I need to hear it.

*   **The "When It's Done" Rule**
    I dislike the phrase "it'll be done when it's done." I always provide dates and ETAs for my actions, and I expect the same from you. If you truly don't know when something will be finished, tell me honestly so we can figure it out together.

*   **Ownership**
    I treat every assigned task as my own by understanding the "why" behind it and the importance of the deadline. This mindset helps me do my best work. If you don't know why you're doing a task, please ask. The last thing I want is for you to do something just because I said so.

*   **[Husnuzan](http://www.ijhssnet.com/journals/Vol_4_No_6_April_2014/10.pdf) (Positive Assumption)**
    I give my trust from day one. To lead effectively, I choose to put a positive spin on everything that happens. This helps me find the silver lining, maintain psychological safety, and focus on learning from mistakes rather than assigning blame.

### **My Expectations for You**
We will have a 1:1 every week for at least 30 minutes, except during high-pressure periods (like audits or critical incidents). We can revisit the frequency later, but I prefer to over-communicate early on. I'll share what I'm working on, the problems I'm facing, and updates from the broader organization. If I forget to share these, please nudge me.

Please be proactive. If you sense something is off—within your team, another team, or a specific situation—tell me right away. When in doubt, over-communicate.

Keep sharpening your saw. Focus on software engineering fundamentals and stay curious about new tech. Trends change, but solid fundamentals make it easy to catch up on anything.

Build your network with **[Habluminannas](http://hrmars.com/hrmars_papers/The_Application_of_Habluminallah_and_Habluminannas_in_the_Work_Motivation_of_the_Management.pdf)** in mind. Engaging with the community and meeting new peers will broaden your perspective. I’m happy to help you find groups that fit your career goals.

### **If You Are a People Manager**

*   **Priority 1:1s:** Ensure you have weekly 1:1s with your direct reports. Be explicit about their career plans and feedback. 
*   **Coach & Context:** Share context from the org (which I will always try to provide to you) and coach them through their challenges.
*   **Constant Learning:** Continue to improve your management skills through conferences, workshops, and peer learning. People management is tricky; people change, and you need a variety of tools to support them.
*   **Work-Life Harmony:** Promote balance for yourself and your team. Take your vacations, pursue your hobbies, and stay active. I believe people who maintain this balance are the ones who truly get things done.

I will occasionally hold skip-level 1:1s with your direct reports. This isn't about micromanagement—it's about staying connected to what’s happening on the ground and building relationships across the team.

### **Meetings**
Meeting hell is real, and I try to avoid it. You should too. I may reject an invite if I don't feel I can contribute, but please keep me in the loop via the meeting notes (MoM). 
*   **Agendas:** Every meeting must have an agenda in the invite.
*   **Roles:** Be clear on who is facilitating vs. leading.
*   **Duration:** Keep them short. I prefer 25, 50, or 80-minute blocks. Never go over 80 minutes.

### **Questions & Agenda Banks**

_curated from various sources_

**Typical Questions for 1:1**
*   Human learning and growth requires the right amount of 4 things: new challenges, low ego, space to reflect + brainstorm, timely + clear feedback. Is there one you need more/less of?
*   What are your goals for this year? And for the next 3 months?
*   What do you need from your manager?
*   What do you need from your team?
*   What do you need from your peers? (outside the team)
*   What did you do this weekend?
*   Do you have any fun trips coming up?
*   What's your top priority for the week?
*   What's holding you back at work right now?
*   What are you struggling to accomplish by the end of the quarter?
*   How can I help you?

**Typical Team Meeting Agenda**
*   Company news
    *   What are you unclear about?
    *   How does what you're working on impact our OKR?
*   Wins & Failures from the team
    *   What worked this month? (why)
    *   What didn't? (why)
*   Project Updates
    *   How are you tracking toward your goals?
    *   Do you need help?
*   Guest Update from a different department
    *   What are you working on?
    *   How does that impact our team?
    *   How can we help?

**Typical Group Problem Solving Agenda**
*   Discuss the issue and brainstorm solutions
    *   What's the issue?
    *   Why is this happening?
    *   What outcome do we want?
    *   How will we achieve that outcome?
*   Decide on a way forward, and divvy up the tasks
    *   Which solution is best? [tip: get people to vote]
    *   What are the tasks that need to get done to achieve that solution?
    *   Who's doing what?
*   Set goals
    *   When will each task be done?
    *   What does success look like?
